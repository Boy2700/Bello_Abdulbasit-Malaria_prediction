PREVENTION OF MALAIRA

Environmental Management

  • Eliminate or destroy mosquito breeding sites and conduct general clean-up of bushes and tall grasses where mosquitoes live.
  • Empty standing water at least once a week to prevent mosquitoes from laying eggs.

Personal Protection

  • Use insect repellents with active ingredients such as DEET, picaridin, IR3535, oil of lemon eucalyptus, para-menthane-diol, and 2-undecanon.
  • Wear loose-fitting, long-sleeved shirts and pants.
  • Use 0.5% permethrin to treat clothing and gear, such as boots, pants, socks, and tents, or buy permethrin-treated clothing and gear.
  • Do not use permethrin products directly on skin.

Travelers' Precautions

  • Before traveling to an area where malaria is common, consult a healthcare provider about malaria prevention medication.
  • While traveling, sleep under insecticide-treated nets.
  • Prevent mosquito bites by following the steps listed above.

Community-Based Prevention

  • Implement indoor residual spraying to kill adult mosquitoes in homes.
  • Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets and house screening to prevent adult mosquito biting in homes.

Public Health Measures

  • Conduct public health campaigns to educate people about the risks of malaria and how to prevent it.
  • Implement vector control measures such as larval control and adult mosquito control.

Vaccination

  • Administer the malaria vaccine to children who live in areas where malaria is endemic.

Treatment and Follow-up

  • Seek medical care immediately if symptoms of malaria occur after traveling to an area where malaria is common.
  • Follow up with healthcare providers to ensure treatment compliance and monitor for any new malaria infections.
